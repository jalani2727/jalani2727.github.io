About Me

Tl;DR / Full Story

I graduated from Emory in 2017, got an entry-level job that wasn't for me, and left three months later. I started my career to pursue what I had studied, but soon realized my education wasn’t over. I needed to find a way to use my passion for programming and work in a creative, collaborative environment.

After combing my brain for a few seconds in search of a purpose, I remembered that my desire to study computer science in high school was trumped by imaginary pressure from my parents to pursue what they had wanted for me. So, I reached out to people I knew on social media who knew how to code! Luckily, the great Bhisma Bakhai responded.

Among other common interests, we found that neither of us wanted to go back to school in order to become the expert coders we are today. Together we looked for alternatives and found DigitalCrafts, a coding bootcamp. There I learned many of the techniques and approaches to coding that I possess today and met some of the kindest instructors and peers!

Now, I strive to adapt my brain in pace with today's ever-changing technologies and programming languages so I can keep building projects that are almost as awesome as this portfolio.
